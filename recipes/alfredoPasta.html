Alfredo Pasta

Description: Alfredo pasta is a rich and creamy Italian dish traditionally made with fettuccine noodles, butter, and Parmesan cheese. The sauce is smooth and velvety, coating the pasta evenly. It's often garnished with freshly ground black pepper and parsley, making it a comforting and indulgent meal.

  • 12 oz (340g) fettuccine pasta
  • 1/2 cup (1 stick) unsalted butter
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1 cup freshly grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2-3 cloves garlic, minced (optional)
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish, optional)

Instructions:

  1. Cook the Pasta:
    • In a large pot of salted boiling water, cook the fettuccine according to package instructions until al dente.
    • Reserve about 1/2 cup of pasta cooking water, then drain and set aside.
  2. Make the Alfredo Sauce:
    • In a large skillet over medium heat, melt the butter.
    • If using garlic, add it to the melted butter and saut√© for about 1 minute until fragrant.
    • Pour in the heavy cream and bring to a simmer. Cook for 2-3 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the cream has slightly thickened.
  3. Combine and Finish:
    • Reduce the heat to low and gradually add the Parmesan cheese, stirring continuously until the cheese is fully melted and the sauce is smooth.
    • Season with salt and black pepper to taste.
  4. Toss the Pasta:
    • Add the cooked fettuccine to the sauce, tossing to coat the pasta evenly. If the sauce is too thick, add a little of the reserved pasta cooking water to reach your desired consistency.
  5. Serve:
    • Transfer the pasta to serving plates or bowls. Garnish with chopped parsley if desired, and serve immediately.
